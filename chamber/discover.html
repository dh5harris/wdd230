Quilt Museum

Paducah is home to the National Quilt Museum of the American Quilter's Society. Paducah also hosts an annual Quilt show on the third weekend on April.

The Carson Center

Located in downtown Paducah, The Carson Center for the Preforming Arts is the area's place for live music performances, broadway plays, and educational experiences.

The Market House Theatre

The Market House Theatre, located on Market House Square in the historic part of downtown Paducah. If you enjoy plays, The Market House Theatre is something you will enjoy.

Land Between the Lakes

A short drive away is Land Between the Lakes. Land Between the Lakes is a National Recreation Area that covers 170,000 acres that features camping, hiking, hunting, and has a Bison herd.

Fort Massac

Across the Ohio River from Paducah, is Metropolis, IL. It is not just the home of Superman, it is also home to Fort Massac State Park. There has been several forts build there. The most notable one was by the French.

Paducah's Wall to Wall

Paducah's Wall to Wall is a series of murals on the flood wall. These murals depict the history of Paducah. They were painted by painter Robert Dafford.

Historic Downtown

Historic Downtown has many shops, resturants, museums, arts, and more.

West Kentucky Community and Technical College

Paducah is home to West Kentucky Community and Technical College. WKCTC has been named a finalist for the Apsen Prize for Community College program, making it one of the top community colleges in the nation five of the last six times.

Noble Park

Noble Park is the main and largest park in Paducah. It is 135 acres. The park host 5k runs, has a swimming pool, tennis, basketball courts, skate park, ball fields and more.
